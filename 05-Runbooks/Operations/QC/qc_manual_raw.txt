1. Introduction

1.1 Purpose and Scope
This Quality Control Manual establishes the policies, procedures, and standards for the manufacture of factory-built housing (FBH) modules at two approved stationary production locations. It complies fully with California Health and Safety Code Division 13, Part 6 and Title 25, California Code of Regulations, Division 1, Chapter 3, Subchapter 1.
Modules are constructed stationary at a single dedicated position on open land with covered workspaces. All labor is performed by pre-qualified specialty subcontractors. Primary structural elements use Steel C-beam foundations and ready-to-assemble Structural Insulated Panels (SIPs).
This single manual governs both locations simultaneously. Identical processes, materials, QC procedures, and documentation apply at both sites.

1.2 Quality Policy Statement
Home Genii Development LLC is committed to manufacturing high-quality factory-built housing that complies with all California regulations and exceeds customer expectations. Quality is achieved through defect prevention. Management ensures this QCM is understood and implemented at both locations. All personnel and subcontractors are accountable for quality. Continuous improvement is a core value, supported by quarterly internal QC audits at both sites.

1.3 Definitions and Terminology
Factory-Built Housing (FBH): Per HSC §19971.
Module: Transportable section built stationary at one position.
Stationary Production Position: Fixed pad on open land with covered workspace.
QAA and DAA: HCD-certified per Title 25 §3010.

2. Facility Description (Two Locations)

2.1 Facility Overview
Both locations use identical stationary single-position production. Modules are built start-to-finish at one fixed pad per module. Covered workspaces protect weather-sensitive operations.
Location 1: 1930 W Whitesbridge Ave Fresno, CA 93706

2.2 Production Layout
Stable leveling stands for Steel C-beam foundations.

3. Regulatory Compliance
The facility complies with HSC Division 13, Part 6, Title 25 CCR, and all applicable Title 24 codes (Building, Residential, Electrical, Mechanical, Plumbing, Energy, Green Building Standards).
HCD Insignia of Approval is affixed to every module prior to shipment. Local jurisdiction requirements (zoning, snow/wind loads, setbacks) are incorporated via DAA-approved plans.

4. Quality Control Organization

4.1 Structure
The QC Department operates independently of production at both factories. The Responsible Corporate Officer oversees the program for both sites.
Key Roles (identical at both locations):
Quality Control Manager: Overall program administration, regulatory liaison, training, continuous improvement.
Quality Control Inspectors: In-process and final inspections.
Material Coordinator: Incoming verification, storage, traceability.
Document Control Specialist: Plan management, revisions, records.

4.2 Authority and Independence
QC personnel have authority to halt production at either factory for non-conformance. The QC Manager reports directly to senior management.

4.3 Subcontractor Control
All labor is performed by pre-qualified specialty subcontractors.
Pre-qualification: Insurance, experience in specialty, safety records.
Approved Subcontractor List maintained at both locations.
Daily manufacturer QC oversight: pre-work briefings, factory supervision, sign-offs on inspection forms.
Subcontractors receive model-specific instructions and QCM training.
Poor performers are removed from the approved list.


4.4 Unit Identification
Each module receives a unique serial number with location prefix (HG1-XXXXX for Location 1; HG2-XXXXX for Location 2). The serial number is permanently marked on the Steel C-beam foundation and recorded on all forms.

10. Shipping and Delivery
Modules ship at 70-95% completion. Pre Fabricated components flat packed and shipped per project requirements. Pre-shipment requirements include final QC approval, QAA approval, and HCD Insignia attachment.

10.1 HCD Insignia of Approval and Certification Process
QAA performs final in-plant inspection.
QC Manager verifies all systems and tests.
HCD Insignia is affixed in the exact location shown on DAA-approved plans.
Required data imprinted: manufacturer name, model number, serial number, plan approval number, date of manufacture, wind/seismic design loads.
Certificate of Compliance is issued and included with delivery documents.

11. Training Requirements
Initial and ongoing training for all personnel and subcontractors on this QCM, codes, safety, and model-specific plans. Inspector qualifications verified annually.

12. Records and Documentation
Complete quality records retained for a minimum of five (5) years. Records retrievable within 24 hours. The document control system tracks all revisions.
